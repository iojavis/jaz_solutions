Why Tanzania is attracting many investors

  1. Home
  2. Why Tanzania?

Tanzania is becoming a prime destination for investors due to several key factors

Excellent Strategic Market Access

Tanzania provides access to over 450 million consumers in Eastern and Southern Africa through preferential trade agreements. In addition, Tanzania qualifies to export 6,000+ products to the U.S. under AGOA and has trade partnerships with China, Canada, and Japan. As a member of EAC and SADC, Tanzania benefits from regional trade opportunities.

Geographical Advantage

Located on the Indian Ocean, Tanzania serves as a trade hub for six landlocked countries (DRC, Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, Zambia, and Malawi). Tanzania has three deepwater ports (Dar es Salaam, Tanga, and Mtwara) facilitating international trade. That notwithstanding, the country has strong rail and road networks enhance connectivity within Africa.

Political & Economic Stability

Tanzania ranks among the 20 fastest-growing economies, with a 7% GDP growth rate over the past decade. The country transitioned from a centrally planned economy to a market-oriented system, encouraging private sector-led growth. It is one of Africa’s most peaceful nations, with a history of free and fair elections since independence in 1961.

Investment Guarantees

Tanzania offers legal protections for investors through BITs, BTAs, MIGA, ATIA, and ICSID agreements.

Rich Natural Resources

The country boasts a long coastline, arable land, and abundant minerals (gold, silver, coal, iron ore, nickel, and gemstones). Oil and gas reserve provide significant economic opportunities, with favorable conditions support agriculture, agro-processing, livestock, and leather production. Tanzania’s strategic location, stable economy, investor-friendly policies, and natural wealth make it an attractive destination for global investors.
