Why Tanzania is attracting many investors

Tanzania is becoming a prime destination for investors due to several key factors

Excellent Strategic Market Access

Tanzania provides access to over 450 million consumers in Eastern and Southern Africa through preferential trade agreements. In addition, Tanzania qualifies to export 6,000+ products to the U.S. under AGOA and has trade partnerships with China, Canada, and Japan. As a member of EAC and SADC, Tanzania benefits from regional trade opportunities.

Geographical Advantage

Located on the Indian Ocean, Tanzania serves as a trade hub for six landlocked countries (DRC, Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, Zambia, and Malawi). Tanzania has three deepwater ports (Dar es Salaam, Tanga, and Mtwara) facilitating international trade. That notwithstanding, the country has strong rail and road networks enhance connectivity within Africa.

Political & Economic Stability

Tanzania ranks among the 20 fastest-growing economies, with a 7% GDP growth rate over the past decade. The country transitioned from a centrally planned economy to a market-oriented system, encouraging private sector-led growth. It is one of Africa’s most peaceful nations, with a history of free and fair elections since independence in 1961.

Investment Guarantees

Tanzania offers legal protections for investors through BITs, BTAs, MIGA, ATIA, and ICSID agreements.

Rich Natural Resources

The country boasts a long coastline, arable land, and abundant minerals (gold, silver, coal, iron ore, nickel, and gemstones). Oil and gas reserve provide significant economic opportunities, with favorable conditions support agriculture, agro-processing, livestock, and leather production. Tanzania’s strategic location, stable economy, investor-friendly policies, and natural wealth make it an attractive destination for global investors.



Tanzania's Economic Growth and Stability (2022-2025): A Comprehensive Analysis

Tanzania has demonstrated resilient economic performance over the past three years, navigating global shocks while maintaining macroeconomic stability and accelerating growth. Key developments are analyzed below:

1. GDP Growth Acceleration

Tanzania's economy rebounded from 4.7% growth in 2022 to 5.1% in 2023 and 5.9% in 2024, with projections of 6.1% in 2025 and 6.4% in 2026. This steady recovery surpasses the Sub-Saharan African average and reflects the diversified sectoral momentum

2. Sectoral Growth Drivers
  • ICT & Digital Economy: Fastest-growing sector (12.5% in 2024), fueled by mobile money adoption and digital infrastructure.
  • Energy: Expanded 11.0% (2024) due to natural gas investments and the near-completion of the 2,115 MW Julius Nyerere Hydropower Project
  • Mining: Grew 8.6% (2024), with gold dominating exports. New projects (e.g., Kabanga Nickel, Nyanzaga gold) attracted $667M in 2023 investments.
  • Tourism: Rebounded to 5.8% growth (2024) after pandemic lows
3. External Sector Dynamics
  • Trade & Current Account: Current account deficit narrowed sharply from 7.5% of GDP (2022) to 2.6% (2024), aided by rising tourism and mineral exports . Merchandise exports reached $9.2B (2024), though a 1.5% dip preceded projected 6.0% rebound in 2025
  • Investment Inflows: FDI surged to $3.5B (2023/24), driven by mining, energy, and manufacturing. The Tanzania Investment Act (2022) reduced bureaucracy and guaranteed profit repatriation
4. Future Outlook and priorities
  • Growth Catalysts: Vision 2050 and infrastructure projects (e.g., Standard Gauge Railway, Lobito Corridor) will enhance regional trade. Mining and energy investments could double mineral earnings to $6.6B by 2027

Tanzania has achieved remarkable macroeconomic stability alongside accelerating growth, positioning itself as East Africa's emerging economic powerhouse. With consistent policy reforms and strategic infrastructure, Tanzania is poised to become a regional leader.
